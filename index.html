Our Story

Born From
Devotion & Duty

Shree Gau Dham Gaushala was founded in 2006 by a small group of devoted volunteers who could not bear to see the sacred cow abandoned, injured, or left to wander hungry streets.

What began as a humble shelter for a few dozen cows has grown into a thriving sanctuary spanning over 12 acres, housing more than 1,200 cows — including old, injured, diseased, and abandoned animals who would otherwise have nowhere to go.

We operate entirely on the goodwill of our community. Every rupee donated goes directly toward feed, veterinary care, and the maintenance of our animals.

Discover Our Mission

What We Do

Our Sacred Mission

Every action we take is rooted in compassion, seva, and the ancient Indian tradition of Gau Seva.

🏠

Shelter & Safety

We provide clean, spacious, and comfortable shelters for all cows — regardless of their age, health, or breed. No cow is ever turned away.

🌿

Nutrition & Feed

Our cows receive nutritious, balanced meals three times a day — fresh fodder, hay, grain, and mineral supplements — sourced from local farmers.

⚕️

Veterinary Care

Our resident veterinarians and trained staff provide daily health checkups, treatment for illness, and emergency surgical care round the clock.

🌱

Organic Farming

Cow dung and urine are used to create organic fertilizers and panchagavya products, supporting sustainable agriculture in our region.

📚

Education & Awareness

We run community programs, school visits, and workshops to spread awareness about cow protection and the importance of Gau Seva.

🤝

Community Seva

Volunteers from all walks of life come together to serve at our gaushala — feeding, cleaning, and loving our sacred residents.
